Here are some key points you could consider including:
Location
This plant is in the best location for local farmers. The majority of feedstock taken by Ashton Keynes Green Energy will come from a 15km radius. Farmers who put the food on your plates are going to be the ones who benefit.
Farming Benefits
The plans support local farmers and the rural economy at a time when things have never been harder. Ashton Keynes Green Energy will use organic materials from farmers to feed the plant and provide natural fertiliser in return to give farms the stability they need.
Homegrown Energy
The plans will produce green energy and support energy security. Biomethane produced will help our transition to net zero. This reduces our dependence on foreign gas supplies. The plant will also sequester CO2 which will address national shortages.
Traffic
Ashton Keynes Green Energy will have no unacceptable impact on local traffic. Nearly 50% of traffic going to the site will be redistributed farming traffic. This means that there will only be a 1% maximum increase on existing traffic.
Data protection: This information is being collected by Quatro PR on behalf of Acorn Bioenergy. Your email will be sent to all members of Wiltshire Council Strategic Planning Committee and to Quatro PR.
Why this matters
  • Supports local farmers within a 15km radius with feedstock contracts and natural fertiliser
  • Produces green biomethane to cut dependence on foreign gas and help reach net zero
  • Only a 1% maximum increase in local traffic — nearly half of deliveries are redistributed farm trips
  • Sequesters CO₂, addressing national shortages
